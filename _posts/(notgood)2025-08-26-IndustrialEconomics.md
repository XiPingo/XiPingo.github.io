# Course Introduction

<mark>Course number: 09000510 Credit: 3</mark>   

The teaching content of this course is as follows:   

<table border="1" width="100%">
  <tr>
    <th>Week</th>
    <th>Content</th>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td>1-2: Introduction to Industrial Economics & Industrial Policy</td>
    <td>
      <ul>
        <li>Industry and industrial economics</li>
        <li>Industrial policy</li>
        <li>Relationship between industrial policy and market mechanism</li>
      </ul>
    </td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td>3-5: Industrial Structure Analysis</td>
    <td>
      <ul>
        <li>Industrial classification and industrial structure</li>
        <li>Basic methods of industrial structure analysis</li>
        <li>Overall trends of industrial structure changes</li>
        <li>Theoretical explanations for the drivers of industrial structure changes</li>
        <li>Economic growth effects of industrial structure changes</li>
        <li>Selection and cultivation of leading industries</li>
      </ul>
    </td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td>6-8: Industrial Linkage Analysis & Industrial Structure Policy</td>
    <td>
      <ul>
        <li>Basic principles of input-output method</li>
        <li>Input-output models</li>
        <li>Analysis of industrial linkage effects</li>
        <li>Analysis of industrial ripple effects</li>
        <li>Dynamic analysis of industrial linkages</li>
        <li>Industrial structure policy</li>
      </ul>
    </td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td>9: Industrial Organization Analysis & Market Structure</td>
    <td>
      <ul>
        <li>Methods of industrial organization analysis</li>
        <li>Market structure analysis</li>
        <li>Application of enterprise competitive strategy in market structure analysis</li>
      </ul>
    </td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td>10: Competition and Cooperation in Oligopolistic Markets</td>
    <td>
      <ul>
        <li>Non-cooperative oligopolistic markets</li>
        <li>Cooperative oligopolistic markets</li>
      </ul>
    </td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td>11: Strategic Pricing Behavior of Enterprises</td>
    <td>
      <ul>
        <li>Entry-deterring pricing</li>
        <li>Predatory pricing</li>
        <li>Price discrimination</li>
        <li>Price wars</li>
      </ul>
    </td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td>12: Non-Price Competition: Advertising</td>
    <td>
      <ul>
        <li>Advertising and its mechanism of influence on consumers</li>
        <li>Advertising budget</li>
        <li>Market effects of advertising</li>
      </ul>
    </td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td>13-14: Non-Price Competition: Technological Progress</td>
    <td>
      <ul>
        <li>Connotation and forms of technological progress</li>
        <li>Stimuli and obstacles to inventions in market economy</li>
        <li>Relationship between market structure and invention/innovation</li>
        <li>Determinants of the diffusion speed of new technologies</li>
        <li>Analysis of technological progress mechanism of Chinese enterprises in transition period</li>
        <li>Empirical study on technological innovation of manufacturing enterprises in Jiangsu, China</li>
      </ul>
    </td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td>15: Non-Price Competition: Mergers and Acquisitions (M&A)</td>
    <td>
      <ul>
        <li>Connotation and forms of M&A</li>
        <li>Drivers of mergers</li>
        <li>Rules for successful M&A</li>
      </ul>
    </td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td>16: Non-Price Competition: Vertical Integration & Vertical Restraints</td>
    <td>
      <ul>
        <li>Strategic benefits and costs of vertical integration</li>
        <li>Vertical disintegration and measurement of vertical integration degree</li>
        <li>Vertical restraints</li>
      </ul>
    </td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td>17: Market Performance & Industrial Organization Policy</td>
    <td>
      <ul>
        <li>Analysis of market performance</li>
        <li>Connotation and theoretical basis of industrial organization policy</li>
        <li>Anti-monopoly policy</li>
        <li>Government regulation and deregulation</li>
      </ul>
    </td>
  </tr>
</table>
