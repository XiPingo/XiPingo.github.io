# Course Introduction   

Course number: 00010011C Credit: 4   

The teaching content of this course is as follows:   

<table border="1" width="100%"> <tr> <th>Section</th> <th>Content</th> </tr> <tr> <td>1. Determinants</td> <td> <ul> <li>Substitution elimination for linear systems, 2nd & 3rd-order determinants</li> <li>Inversion number of permutations and its properties</li> <li>Definition of general n-order determinant via inversion number</li> <li>Basic properties of n-order determinants</li> </ul> </td> </tr> <tr> <td>2. Matrix Fundamentals & Cramer's Rule</td> <td> <ul> <li>Cofactors and algebraic cofactors</li> <li>Laplace expansion (cofactor expansion) for n-order determinants</li> <li>Calculation examples for determinants</li> <li>Cramer's Rule for n×n linear systems</li> <li>Basic concepts of matrices and vectors</li> </ul> </td> </tr> <tr> <td>3. Matrix Operations & Elementary Matrices</td> <td> <ul> <li>Definition of matrix operations and their algebraic laws</li> <li>Verification: Associativity of matrix multiplication, transpose of product</li> <li>Block matrices</li> <li>Elementary row operations and elementary matrices</li> <li>Fundamental relationship between elementary operations and matrices</li> </ul> </td> </tr> <tr> <td>4. Matrix Rank & Invertibility</td> <td> <ul> <li>Concept of matrix rank</li> <li>Effect of elementary operations on rank</li> <li>Invertible matrices and adjugate matrices</li> <li>Properties of invertible matrices</li> <li>Relationship between a square matrix and its adjugate</li> <li>Necessary and sufficient condition for invertibility</li> <li>Finding inverse via adjugate matrix</li> </ul> </td> </tr> <tr> <td>5. Vector Spaces I: Linear Dependence & Rank</td> <td> <ul> <li>Elementary transformation method for inverse</li> <li>Matrix decomposition theorem</li> <li>Decomposition theorem for invertible matrices</li> <li>Rank of product with invertible matrix</li> <li>Linear combination, linear dependence/independence</li> <li>Methods to determine dependence/independence</li> <li>Relationship between linear dependence and matrix rank</li> <li>Maximal linearly independent set (basis), rank of a vector set</li> <li>Rank relations under equivalent vector sets</li> <li>Matrix rank vs. row/column vector set ranks</li> <li>Rank of matrix sum and product</li> </ul> </td> </tr> <tr> <td>6. Linear Systems I: Solvability</td> <td> <ul> <li>Basic concepts of linear systems</li> <li>Necessary and sufficient conditions for solution existence</li> </ul> </td> </tr> <tr> <td>7. Linear Systems II: Structure & Solution</td> <td> <ul> <li>Fundamental solution set for homogeneous systems</li> <li>Particular solution, general solution, solution structure under infinite solutions</li> <li>Solution methods</li> <li>Relationship between nonhomogeneous system solutions and solutions of its homogeneous counterpart</li> <li>Finding general solution for nonhomogeneous systems</li> <li>Matrix similarity concept</li> </ul> </td> </tr> <tr> <td>8. Eigenvalues & Eigenvectors I</td> <td> <ul> <li>Introduction to eigenvalues and eigenvectors</li> <li>Characteristic polynomial, equation, matrix</li> <li>Methods to find eigenvalues</li> <li>Relationship between eigenvectors and fundamental solution sets</li> <li>Invariants under matrix similarity (proofs)</li> </ul> </td> </tr> <tr> <td>9. Eigenvalues & Eigenvectors II: Diagonalization</td> <td> <ul> <li>Necessary and sufficient conditions for matrix diagonalizability</li> <li>Eigenvectors corresponding to distinct eigenvalues (linear independence)</li> <li>Maximal independent set of eigenvectors as union over eigenvalues</li> <li>Relationship between algebraic multiplicity and geometric multiplicity</li> </ul> </td> </tr> <tr> <td>10. Orthogonality & Real Symmetric Matrices</td> <td> <ul> <li>Inner product, orthogonal vector sets</li> <li>Gram-Schmidt orthogonalization process</li> <li>Orthogonal matrices: concept and properties</li> <li>Diagonalization of real symmetric matrices</li> </ul> </td> </tr> <tr> <td>11. Quadratic Forms</td> <td> <ul> <li>Real quadratic forms: concept, matrix representation, standard form</li> <li>Methods: Completing the square, eigenvalue method</li> <li>Congruent transformations, canonical form, inertia indices, connection to eigenvalues</li> </ul> </td> </tr> <tr> <td>12. Linear Spaces & Positive Definiteness</td> <td> <ul> <li>Positive definite quadratic forms: concept and criteria</li> <li>Linear space: definition, basis, dimension, coordinates</li> <li>Change of basis and coordinate transformation</li> </ul> </td> </tr> <tr> <td>13. Subspaces & Linear Transformations</td> <td> <ul> <li>Subspaces of linear spaces</li> <li>Linear transformations: definition</li> <li>Matrix representation of linear transformations</li> </ul> </td> </tr> <tr> <td>14. Eigen Theory for Linear Transformations</td> <td> <ul> <li>Eigenvalues and eigenvectors of linear transformations</li> <li>Simplest matrix representation (Jordan form implied)</li> </ul> </td> </tr> <tr> <td>15. Inner Product Spaces</td> <td> <ul> <li>Inner product, norm, angle between vectors</li> <li>Orthogonal sets, Euclidean spaces, orthogonal basis</li> <li>Orthogonal transformations</li> </ul> </td> </tr> <tr> <td>16. Isomorphism & Review</td> <td> <ul> <li>Isomorphism of Euclidean spaces</li> <li>(Final Exam Review)</li> </ul> </td> </tr> </table>
