# Course Introduction

The teaching content of this course is as follows:   

<table border="1" width="100%"> <tr> <th>Section</th> <th>Content</th> </tr> <tr> <td>1. Introduction to Economic Development</td> <td> <ul> <li>Definition and scope of economic development</li> <li>Global patterns of income, growth, and inequality</li> <li>Key development indicators and data sources</li> </ul> </td> </tr> <tr> <td>2. Comparative Economic Development</td> <td> <ul> <li>Historical and regional development experiences</li> <li>Structural differences between developed and developing economies</li> <li>Convergence, divergence, and development paths</li> </ul> </td> </tr> <tr> <td>3. Classic Theories of Economic Growth and Development</td> <td> <ul> <li>Classical growth theories and structural change models</li> <li>Lewis dual-sector model and surplus labor</li> <li>Harrod–Domar and early growth frameworks</li> </ul> </td> </tr> <tr> <td>4. Empirical Evidence on Economic Growth</td> <td> <ul> <li>Stylized facts of economic growth</li> <li>Empirical testing of growth models</li> <li>Role of institutions, geography, and policy</li> </ul> </td> </tr> <tr> <td>5. Contemporary Models of Development and Underdevelopment</td> <td> <ul> <li>Endogenous growth theory</li> <li>Multiple equilibria and poverty traps</li> <li>Coordination failures and institutional constraints</li> </ul> </td> </tr> <tr> <td>6. Poverty, Inequality, and Development</td> <td> <ul> <li>Measurement of poverty and inequality</li> <li>Income distribution and growth</li> <li>Policy interventions and redistribution</li> </ul> </td> </tr> <tr> <td>7. Population Growth and Economic Development</td> <td> <ul> <li>Demographic transition theory</li> <li>Population growth and economic outcomes</li> <li>Fertility, mortality, and development policy</li> </ul> </td> </tr> <tr> <td>8. Urbanization and Rural–Urban Migration</td> <td> <ul> <li>Migration theories and labor mobility</li> <li>Urbanization patterns in developing countries</li> <li>Policy challenges of urban growth</li> </ul> </td> </tr> <tr> <td>9. Human Capital: Education and Health</td> <td> <ul> <li>Human capital theory</li> <li>Education, health, and productivity</li> <li>Public investment and development outcomes</li> </ul> </td> </tr> <tr> <td>10. Agricultural Transformation and Rural Development</td> <td> <ul> <li>Role of agriculture in development</li> <li>Productivity, technology, and rural markets</li> <li>Rural development strategies</li> </ul> </td> </tr> <tr> <td>11. Environment and Sustainable Development</td> <td> <ul> <li>Environment–development trade-offs</li> <li>Natural resource management</li> <li>Sustainable development policies</li> </ul> </td> </tr> <tr> <td>12. Development Policymaking and Institutions</td> <td> <ul> <li>Role of markets, states, and civil society</li> <li>Institutions and governance</li> <li>Policy design and implementation challenges</li> </ul> </td> </tr> <tr> <td>13. Foreign Finance, Investment, and Aid</td> <td> <ul> <li>Foreign direct investment and development</li> <li>Foreign aid: debates and effectiveness</li> <li>International capital flows</li> </ul> </td> </tr> <tr> <td>14. Finance and Fiscal Policy for Development</td> <td> <ul> <li>Financial systems in developing economies</li> <li>Fiscal capacity and public finance</li> <li>Macroeconomic policy and development</li> </ul> </td> </tr> <tr> <td>15. Course Review</td> <td> <ul> <li>Integration of development theories and evidence</li> <li>Policy implications and open questions</li> <li>Preparation for final assessment</li> </ul> </td> </tr> </table>
